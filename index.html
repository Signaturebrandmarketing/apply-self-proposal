You created the SELF Framework — Strength-Based Ecological Leadership — to translate your doctoral research into leadership growth that honors a person's strengths, context, and identity. It rejects deficit narratives and one-size-fits-all checklists. It meets leaders where they actually lead.

The challenge every framework like yours faces is the same: how do you scale something this personal and reflective beyond the room you're standing in? Workshops, retreats, and consulting are powerful — and limited by your calendar. That's the problem we want to solve with you.

The idea

Your four pillars already describe a simulation.

A leadership simulation is the most faithful possible delivery of SELF. Look how naturally each pillar becomes part of the experience.

PILLAR 01

Strength-Based Foundations

Apply strengths, don't fix deficits.
The player leads from their strengths — choices that leverage strengths advance the story; generic checklist choices fall flat.
PILLAR 02

Ecological Systems Thinking

Context shapes leadership.
The simulation lives in a real environment — a center, a school, a team — and the same decision plays out differently by context.
PILLAR 03

Reflective Practice

Self-awareness, built into routine.
Reflection prompts after each decision are the debrief — your third pillar becomes the heart of how the simulation teaches.
PILLAR 04

Adult Learning Principles

Engaging, practical, applicable.
Experiential, play-based practice is exactly what adults learn best from — your fourth pillar is why this format works.

We're not bolting a game onto your research.

We're putting your research into motion. A leader steps into a living context, leads from their strengths, watches their choices ripple through the system, and reflects — turning insight into practice. That is SELF, made playable and repeatable.

One framework. Thousands of leaders. Endless practice.
